⚡ MISSION 1

Protect Yourself

5 cyber hygiene rules every South African needs to know — with real local examples.

🔐
Use Strong Passwords
Avoid your birthday, "password123", or your school name. Use a mix of letters, numbers and symbols. Example: S0wet0@H3ro!
💡 Most South African hacks happen from weak passwords on banking apps.
📲
Turn ON Two-Factor Auth (MFA)
Enable the extra SMS/app code on your FNB, Capitec, or Gmail account. Even if someone has your password, they can't get in without your phone.
💡 Capitec Bank's app supports biometric + PIN as MFA — use both!
🚫
NEVER Share Your OTP
No bank, NSFAS, SASSA, or Vodacom will ever call you and ask for your One-Time Pin. If someone does — it's a scammer. Hang up immediately.
⚠️ "OTP scams" are the #1 banking fraud method in SA right now.
📶
Don't Bank on Public WiFi
Free WiFi at Maponya Mall or the library is great — but never log into your bank or NSFAS on it. Hackers can see everything on public networks.
💡 Use your mobile data for anything financial. It's worth the data cost.
🔄
Update Your Apps & Phone
Those annoying update notifications? They patch security holes. An old Android phone with outdated apps is an open door for attackers.
💡 Enable auto-updates overnight while your phone charges.
